About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Applied and Computational Mathematics at the University of Crete, with an Integrated M.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki. My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and medical data, with a focus on Causal Modeling and Estimation of Causal Parameters.

Before my doctoral studies, I gained extensive experience in biomedical engineering projects, developing a strong interest in the challenges of medical data analysis. I am particularly passionate about building rigorous algorithmic frameworks that can be applied to real clinical problems.

Outside of research, I enjoy teaching mathematics, collaborating on open-source projects, and exploring new developments at the boundary of statistics and computation.
